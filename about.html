About Lesbians Aloud

Who are Lesbians Aloud

The Management Committee, the networks they are connected with, and the people this project is aimed at are predominantly older Lesbians from across the UK. In the absence of physical lesbian spaces to meet-up in, the main source of lesbian networking seems to be a myriad of facebook groups which come and go.

During the covid lockdown our secretary was involved in holding fortnightly zoom meetings for Lesbians, referred to as LUL - Lesbians under Lockdown. It appeared that the majority of attendees were older Lesbians, many living alone and not in support bubbles. The negative impacts of isolation were prevalent within this group.

At each session women took turns to share their story, along with encouragement to read their favourite poems, sing a song or share other personal arts projects. It became obvious that there was a dearth of specifically lesbian-themed books, art, poetry, music, film etc.

Lesbians Aloud aims to carry on the work started during the covid lockdowns, bringing lesbians together through theatre, poetry, singing and music, in order to create a community they can belong to and enjoy and create the arts together.

To do this, it is the intention of lesbians aloud to create a feeling of community that can then be used to work together to create and perform a variety of events, developing the ideas and the stories of the that join the community, in turn, bringing in more lesbians to our community.

Lesbians Aloud aims to:
  1. To give voice to, and amplify the voices of marginalised lesbians, particularly those who are older, disabled and neurodiverse.
  2. To celebrate and archive lesbian lives.
  3. To empower and encourage lesbians to overcome personal, political and institutional barriers.
  4. To empower and encourage lesbians to develop tools to improve their wellbeing and mental health.
  5. To build a strong and resilient lesbian community.
  6. To understand the Arts as a vehicle for 1-5.
Contact Us

We know that older Lesbians suffer more isolation than our straight sisters and that many of us have additional barriers to full participation, such as access needs arising from financial difficulties, disability, deafness, a high incidence of neurodiversity and the rural isolation already mentioned.

Re-establishing in real life meet ups and connections is challenging and many women feel even more vulnerable and cautious about going to what few public events there were than before Covid. The demographic of older Lesbians also experiences a higher-than-average intersectionality with mental health impacts, deafness, disability and neurodivergence reflected throughout our community.

Knowing this, we were confident that we would be meeting a need for our community if we put a project together to create material about our lives, using the arts, and encouraging women to gather to celebrate and perform together.
